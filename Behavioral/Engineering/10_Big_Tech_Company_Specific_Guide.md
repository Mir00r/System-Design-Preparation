## 📦 Amazon: The Most Behavioral-Intensive Interview

### The 16 Leadership Principles (LPs)

Amazon is UNIQUE: behavioral questions happen in EVERY round, and each interviewer is assigned specific LPs to evaluate.

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╗
║  AMAZON INTERVIEW STRUCTURE:                                     ║
║                                                                  ║
║  📞 Phone Screen (1 hour):                                       ║
║     • 2-3 LP-based questions + technical                         ║
║                                                                  ║
║  🏢 On-Site Loop (4-5 rounds, 5-6 hours):                       ║
║     • EACH round: 2-3 LP questions + technical problem           ║
║     • Bar Raiser: Extra-deep behavioral probing                  ║
║     • Each interviewer owns specific LPs                         ║
║                                                                  ║
║  ⚠️ KEY: One bad behavioral round can = REJECTION even           ║
║          if technical is perfect                                  ║
║                                                                  ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

### The Top 8 Most-Tested LPs (for Engineers):

| # | Leadership Principle | What It Means | Example Question |
|---|---------------------|---------------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Customer Obsession** | Start with the customer, work backwards | "Tell me about a time you prioritized customer needs over internal convenience" |
| 2 | **Ownership** | Think long-term, act on behalf of entire company | "Tell me about a time you went beyond your job description" |
| 3 | **Dive Deep** | Dig into details, verify with data | "Tell me about a time you dug deep to find a root cause" |
| 4 | **Bias for Action** | Speed matters, make reversible decisions fast | "Tell me about a decision you made without complete data" |
| 5 | **Deliver Results** | Focus on key inputs, deliver with quality | "Tell me about a time you delivered under a tight deadline" |
| 6 | **Have Backbone; Disagree and Commit** | Challenge, then commit | "Tell me about disagreeing with a decision, then committing" |
| 7 | **Earn Trust** | Be self-critical, benchmark against the best | "Tell me about a time you earned the trust of your team" |
| 8 | **Learn and Be Curious** | Never stop learning, explore | "Tell me about a time you learned something outside your comfort zone" |

### Amazon Deep-Dive: The Follow-Up Questions ⚠️

Amazon interviewers are trained to ask **drilling follow-ups**:

```
Pattern: "Tell me about..." → Your answer → "Tell me more about..."

Round 1: "Tell me about a time you made a tough decision"
You: [Give STAR answer]

Follow-up 1: "What data did you look at?"
Follow-up 2: "Who disagreed with you?"  
Follow-up 3: "What would you do differently now?"
Follow-up 4: "How did you measure success?"
Follow-up 5: "What was the long-term impact?"

⚠️ They go 4-5 levels deep! Vague stories COLLAPSE under this pressure.
   Only REAL experiences survive Amazon drilling.
```

## 🔍 Google: "Googleyness & Leadership" (GnL)

### What Google Actually Evaluates:

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╗
║  GOOGLE'S 4 EVALUATION PILLARS:                                  ║
║                                                                  ║
║  1. 🧠 General Cognitive Ability (GCA)                           ║
║     → Problem-solving, learning ability, structured thinking     ║
║                                                                  ║
║  2. 🛠️ Role-Related Knowledge (RRK)                              ║
║     → Technical skills specific to the role                      ║
║                                                                  ║
║  3. 👑 Leadership                                                ║
║     → Emergent leadership, not positional                        ║
║     → "Do you step up when needed?"                              ║
║                                                                  ║
║  4. 🌟 Googleyness                                               ║
║     → Culture fit, but specifically:                             ║
║     → Enjoys ambiguity, collaborates well                        ║
║     → Pushes back constructively                                 ║
║     → Does the right thing (ethics/integrity)                    ║
║     → Cares about the team, not just self                        ║
║                                                                  ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

### "Googleyness" Decoded:

| Trait | What It Looks Like | Red Flag |
|-------|--------------------|----------|
| **Thrives in ambiguity** | "I love open-ended problems without clear solutions" | "I need clear requirements before I start" |
| **Collaborative** | "I seek out others' perspectives and build on them" | "I prefer to work alone and present finished work" |
| **Pushes back constructively** | "I challenged the approach with data and alternatives" | "I just went along with it" / "I refused and did my own thing" |
| **Does the right thing** | "I raised an ethical concern even though it was uncomfortable" | Prioritizing speed/results over ethics |
| **Humble & coachable** | "I asked for help when stuck" / "I changed my mind when presented with better data" | "I'm always right" attitude |

### Google Behavioral Questions Pattern:

```
Google tends to ask BROADER questions than Amazon:

✅ "Tell me about a time you showed leadership"
   (Open-ended — YOU choose the story)

✅ "Describe a time when you navigated ambiguity"
   (Tests your comfort with uncertainty)

✅ "How do you handle disagreements on your team?"
   (Tests collaboration style)

❌ Google does NOT ask: "Tell me about a time you 
   showed Customer Obsession" (that's Amazon-style)
```

## 🔵 Meta (Facebook): "Move Fast" Culture

### Meta's Core Values for Engineering:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  META'S ENGINEERING VALUES:                          │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                                                     │
│  1. 🚀 MOVE FAST                                    │
│     → Ship quickly, iterate, don't over-plan        │
│     → "What have you shipped recently?"             │
│                                                     │
│  2. 🔨 BUILD AWESOME THINGS                          │
│     → Technical excellence in service of users      │
│     → "Tell me about something you're proud of"     │
│                                                     │
│  3. 💪 BE BOLD                                       │
│     → Take risks, propose big ideas                 │
│     → "Tell me about a risk you took"               │
│                                                     │
│  4. 🌍 FOCUS ON LONG-TERM IMPACT                     │
│     → Not just quick wins, but lasting value        │
│     → "How did your work impact the company long-term?" │
│                                                     │
│  5. 📖 BE OPEN                                       │
│     → Transparency, sharing information             │
│     → "How do you share knowledge with your team?"  │
│                                                     │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Meta's Behavioral Interview Structure:

```
📞 Phone Screen:
   • 45 min coding + 15 min behavioral

🏢 On-Site:
   • 2 coding rounds
   • 1 system design round  
   • 1 "Behavioral" round (called "Culture Fit" or "Leadership")
   
The behavioral round focuses on:
   → Collaboration & team dynamics
   → Impact and delivery speed
   → How you handle failure
   → Technical decision-making process
```

### Key Meta Questions:

| Question | What They Evaluate |
|----------|-------------------|
| "What's the project you're most proud of?" | Technical depth + pride in craft |
| "Tell me about a time you had to ship something fast" | Speed vs quality balance |
| "How do you handle a project that's not going well?" | Resilience, problem-solving |
| "Describe working with a cross-functional team" | Collaboration style |
| "Tell me about a technical decision you'd make differently" | Self-awareness, growth |

## 💼 Microsoft: Growth Mindset Culture

### Microsoft's Evaluation Framework:

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╗
║  MICROSOFT'S CORE BEHAVIORAL PILLARS:                            ║
║                                                                  ║
║  🌱 GROWTH MINDSET (Satya Nadella's #1 cultural value)           ║
║     → "Learn-it-all" vs "Know-it-all"                           ║
║     → How you handle failure and feedback                        ║
║     → Curiosity and continuous learning                          ║
║                                                                  ║
║  🤝 COLLABORATION                                                ║
║     → "One Microsoft" — working across teams/divisions           ║
║     → Diverse perspectives and inclusive behavior                ║
║                                                                  ║
║  🎯 CUSTOMER-CENTRICITY                                          ║
║     → Building for customer success                              ║
║     → Empathy for user problems                                  ║
║                                                                  ║
║  💡 IMPACT & INNOVATION                                          ║
║     → Driving meaningful results                                 ║
║     → Creative solutions to hard problems                        ║
║                                                                  ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

### Microsoft-Specific Tips:

1. **Emphasize learning from failure** — Microsoft's culture under Nadella is heavily growth-mindset
2. **Show cross-org collaboration** — "One Microsoft" means breaking silos
3. **Demonstrate empathy** — for users, for teammates, for different perspectives
4. **Show innovation** — creative problem-solving, not just executing plans

│     → Nurture and embrace differing perspectives            │

│     → Seek understanding of our strategy and market         │

### Netflix's "Keeper Test":
> 💡 Netflix asks managers: "If this person told you they were leaving, would you fight hard to keep them?" If the answer is no → separation. This means Netflix interviews test if you'd be a "keeper."

## 🎯 The Universal "Story Tailoring" Strategy

### How to Adapt ONE Story for Different Companies:

Take this base story: *"I migrated our monolithic payment service to microservices"*

| Company | Tailored Emphasis |
|---------|------------------|
| **Amazon** | Emphasize: Customer impact, data-driven decisions, ownership mentality, diving deep into metrics |
| **Google** | Emphasize: Ambiguity navigation, collaborative design process, technical rigor, pushing back on bad ideas |
| **Meta** | Emphasize: Speed of delivery, bold decisions, iteration, shipping quickly |
| **Microsoft** | Emphasize: Growth through the challenge, cross-team collaboration, what you learned |
| **Netflix** | Emphasize: Judgment in decisions, impact metrics, courageous technical choices |

### Same Story, Different Framing:

```
🎯 For Amazon (LP: Dive Deep):
"When migrating to microservices, I DIVED DEEP into our traffic 
patterns — analyzing 6 months of logs to identify which service 
boundaries would minimize cross-service calls by 80%..."

🎯 For Google (Googleyness: Ambiguity):
"The migration had NO clear roadmap — we were the first team doing 
this at our scale. I NAVIGATED THE AMBIGUITY by creating a decision 
framework for service boundaries..."

🎯 For Meta (Move Fast):
"Rather than spending months planning, I SHIPPED the first 
microservice in 2 weeks as a proof of concept, then iterated 
based on real production data..."

🎯 For Microsoft (Growth Mindset):
"I had never done a migration at this scale. I LEARNED rapidly — 
studied Netflix's migration patterns, took a DDD course, and 
within 3 weeks was TEACHING the team the approach..."
```
