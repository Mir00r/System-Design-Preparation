## ⭐ The STAR Framework (One-Page Summary)

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╗
║                                                                  ║
║  S (15%) → Set the scene in 2-3 sentences                       ║
║  T (10%) → YOUR specific goal/responsibility                     ║
║  A (60%) → YOUR actions (use "I", explain WHY)                   ║
║  R (15%) → Quantified results + reflection                       ║
║                                                                  ║
║  ⏱️ Target: 2-3 minutes per answer                               ║
║  🎯 Ratio: 70% "I" / 30% "We"                                   ║
║  📊 Always include: at least ONE metric/number                   ║
║  🪞 Always end with: lesson learned or what you'd change         ║
║                                                                  ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

## 🗺️ Question → Framework Quick Map

| When You Hear... | Use This Framework | Key Focus |
|------------------|-------------------|-----------|
| "Tell me about a time you led..." | STAR + Leadership types | YOUR initiative, not title |
| "Describe a conflict..." | STAR + DESC + Steel Man | Bridge-building, not winning |
| "Tell me about a failure..." | STAR+ (with reflection) | Lesson + proof of change |
| "How do you prioritize..." | Eisenhower + RICE + STAR | Framework + specific example |
| "Walk me through debugging..." | PAR (Problem-Action-Result) | Methodology, not just answer |
| "How do you handle ambiguity..." | Disambiguate → Decide → Execute | Comfort with uncertainty |
| "Tell me about going above and beyond..." | STAR + Ownership dimensions | Outcome ownership, not task |
| "How do you handle tight deadlines..." | Scope negotiation + STAR | Cut scope not quality |
| "Describe cross-team collaboration..." | Communication Matrix + STAR | Different languages per audience |
| "What would you do if..." (hypothetical) | Reframe to past experience | "In a similar situation, I..." |

## 🚦 Red Flags to AVOID

| 🔴 Red Flag | Fix |
|------------|-----|
| Vague answers ("things improved") | Quantify: "improved by 65%" |
| Blaming others ("they didn't...") | Own your role: "I should have..." |
| Too long (>4 min) | Practice timing, cut the situation shorter |
| Generic/hypothetical ("I would...") | Specific past event: "Last quarter, I..." |
| No "I" (only "we") | 70% "I" in the action section |
| No reflection | Add: "Looking back, I'd also..." |
| Same story for every question | Prepare 8-10 diverse stories |
| Getting emotional retelling | Choose stories you've processed |
| Memorized-sounding delivery | Practice until natural, not rehearsed |
| No metrics in results | At least one: %, time, $, users, scale |

## ✅ Green Flags That IMPRESS

| 🟢 Green Flag | Example |
|-------------|---------|

## 📅 Last-Minute Preparation Checklist (Day Before)

```
□ Review your 8-10 Story Bank entries (bullet points, not scripts)
□ Practice 3 stories out loud (timing: 2-3 min each)
□ Research the SPECIFIC company's values/principles
□ Prepare 3-5 questions to ASK the interviewer
□ Review the job description — match stories to requirements
□ Sleep well (8+ hours — seriously, sleep > last-minute cramming)
□ Prepare your "Tell me about yourself" (60-90 seconds)
□ Check: Do you have a failure story ready? A conflict story?
□ Check: Can you explain your stories to a non-technical person?
□ Lay out clothes, charge devices, test video/audio (if virtual)
```

## 🗣️ The "Tell Me About Yourself" Formula (60 seconds)

```
1. CURRENT ROLE (15 sec): "I'm currently a [title] at [company], 
   where I [1-2 sentence summary of what you do]."

2. KEY STRENGTH (15 sec): "I specialize in [your niche], 
   and I'm passionate about [what drives you technically]."

3. TOP ACHIEVEMENT (15 sec): "Recently, I [biggest measurable 
   accomplishment that's relevant to this role]."

4. WHY THIS ROLE (15 sec): "I'm excited about [company/role] 
   because [specific reason tied to their mission/challenges]."
```

## 🎮 The "Panic Recovery" Playbook

### When You Draw a Blank:

```
🧊 Stay Calm. Use one of these buy-time phrases:

"That's a great question. Let me think of the best example..."
(Take 5-10 seconds to think. Silence is OK!)

"I have a few examples that come to mind. Let me share the 
most relevant one..."
(This gives you a moment to choose)

"Actually, let me reframe — I think a better example is..."
(It's OK to pivot mid-answer!)
```

### When You're Going Too Long:

```
"I realize I'm going deep on context — let me jump to 
the key actions and results..."
(Self-correct and skip to ACTION)
```

### When You Don't Have a Perfect Example:

```
"I haven't encountered that exact scenario, but the closest 
experience was... [adjacent story that shows the same skills]"
(Transparency + best available example)
```

## 📝 Questions to ASK the Interviewer

### Always Prepare 3-5 Questions. Here Are Great Ones:

| Category | Question | What It Shows |
|----------|----------|--------------|
| 🏗️ **Technical** | "What's the biggest technical challenge your team is facing right now?" | Genuine interest in the work |
| 👥 **Culture** | "How does the team handle disagreements on technical approaches?" | Care about collaboration |
| 🌱 **Growth** | "What does career growth look like for engineers here?" | Long-term thinking |
| 📊 **Impact** | "How do you measure engineering success beyond shipping features?" | Results-oriented |
| 🎯 **Role** | "What would a successful first 90 days look like in this role?" | Eager to contribute |

### ❌ Questions to AVOID:
- "What's the salary?" (Save for recruiter/HR)
- "How many hours do people work?" (Sounds lazy)
- "Can I work remotely full-time?" (Save for offer stage)
- "Did I get the job?" (Puts them in an awkward spot)
